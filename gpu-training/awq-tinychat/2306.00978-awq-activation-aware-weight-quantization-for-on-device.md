# AWQ: Activation-aware Weight Quantization for On-Device LLM Compression and Acceleration

## TL;DR

AWQ proposes activation-aware weight quantization that protects salient weights based on activation distribution, combined with TinyChat inference framework, achieving 4x model compression and 3-4x speedup on edge devices.

## Problem & Motivation

Large language models (LLMs) are too large to run on edge devices due to limited memory and compute. On-device deployment is desirable for reducing cloud costs and preserving privacy, but existing quantization methods degrade accuracy when aggressively compressing models.

## Key Ideas

- Identify salient weights by analyzing activation distributions, not just weight magnitudes.
- Protect only a small fraction (1%) of salient weights by scaling them up before quantization, reducing quantization error.
- TinyChat framework: on-the-fly dequantization, SIMD-aware weight packing, and kernel fusion to realize speedups.

## System Design

AWQ is a quantization algorithm that pre-processes weights by scaling salient channels based on activation statistics. TinyChat is the runtime: it loads quantized weights, performs on-the-fly dequantization during inference, packs weights for SIMD efficiency, and fuses kernels to reduce overhead.

## Evaluation

Evaluated on various LLMs (e.g., LLaMA) across edge platforms from desktop GPUs to IoT devices. Achieves 4x model size reduction and 3-4x speedup with negligible accuracy loss compared to FP16 baselines.

## Takeaways

- Activation distribution is more informative than weight magnitude for identifying important weights.
- Protecting only a tiny fraction of weights (1%) is sufficient to maintain accuracy.
- Algorithm-system co-design is key to translating compression into real speedups.

## Related Work

Compared to uniform quantization and other weight-only quantization methods (e.g., GPTQ, LLM.int8()), AWQ achieves better accuracy-efficiency trade-off by leveraging activation statistics.

## Open Questions

- Extending AWQ to quantize activations as well.
- Applicability to other model architectures beyond transformers.
- Further optimization for extremely resource-constrained devices.
