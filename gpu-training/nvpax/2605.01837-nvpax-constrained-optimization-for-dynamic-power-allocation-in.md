# nvPAX: Constrained Optimization for Dynamic Power Allocation in Hierarchical and Multi-Tenant Systems

## TL;DR

nvPAX is a constrained-optimization policy for dynamic power allocation in hierarchical, multi-tenant datacenters. It uses a three-phase hybrid QP/LP procedure to compute feasible allocations that respect tenant constraints and job priorities, achieving high satisfaction ratios and low runtime.

## Problem & Motivation

As datacenter power density grows, oversubscription becomes necessary to dynamically allocate limited power budgets based on real-time demand. Existing approaches assume flat power domains, but real power distribution is hierarchical, and allocation decisions must also respect tenant-level contractual constraints. This paper addresses the need for a policy that handles both hierarchy and multi-tenancy while maximizing utilization.

## Key Ideas

- Three-phase hybrid QP/LP procedure: Phase I allocates power with minimum deviation from device requests while respecting job priorities; Phase II fairly distributes excess power among active devices; Phase III fairly distributes remaining power to idle devices.
- Formulation as a constrained optimization problem that respects hierarchical power distribution and tenant-level constraints.
- Enables power oversubscription while maximizing datacenter utilization.

## System Design

The system is a control policy that runs at each allocation interval. It takes as input power requests from devices, job priorities, and hierarchical power constraints (including tenant-level caps). The three-phase optimization computes a feasible allocation: Phase I uses quadratic programming (QP) to minimize deviation from requests subject to priority and hierarchy; Phase II and III use linear programming (LP) to distribute excess power fairly among active and idle devices respectively.

## Evaluation

Evaluated via trace-driven large-scale simulation using GPU power telemetry from a production datacenter. nvPAX achieves mean wall-clock time of 264.69 ms per allocation interval and mean satisfaction ratio of 98.92%. It outperforms static equal-share allocation and provides robustness beyond greedy proportional allocation in the presence of non-uniform hierarchical bottlenecks.

## Takeaways

- Hierarchical and multi-tenant constraints are critical for practical power allocation in datacenters.
- A three-phase approach balances request satisfaction, fairness, and utilization.
- nvPAX is computationally efficient (sub-300 ms) and achieves high satisfaction ratios.
- The policy is robust to non-uniform bottlenecks compared to greedy methods.

## Related Work

The paper contrasts with existing approaches that assume flat power domains. It positions nvPAX as addressing the gap for hierarchical and multi-tenant systems, outperforming static equal-share and greedy proportional allocation.

## Open Questions

The paper does not discuss integration with real-time power capping hardware or handling of transient spikes. Future work could explore adaptive phase thresholds or extensions to heterogeneous device types beyond GPUs.
