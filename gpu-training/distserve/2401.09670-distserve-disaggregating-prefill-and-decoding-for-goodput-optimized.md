# DistServe: Disaggregating Prefill and Decoding for Goodput-optimized Large Language Model Serving

## TL;DR

DistServe disaggregates prefill and decoding phases of LLM inference onto separate GPUs, eliminating interference and enabling phase-specific resource/parallelism optimization, achieving up to 7.4x higher request rate or 12.6x tighter SLO under latency constraints.

## Problem & Motivation

Existing LLM serving systems colocate prefill and decoding phases, batching them together across requests. This causes strong interference between the two phases, as prefill is compute-bound and decoding is memory-bound. Moreover, colocation couples resource allocation and parallelism decisions, forcing a one-size-fits-all plan that cannot satisfy both time-to-first-token (TTFT) and time-per-output-token (TPOT) latency requirements simultaneously. To meet stringent SLOs, systems either prioritize one latency over the other or over-provision GPUs, leading to poor goodput (throughput under latency constraints).

## Key Ideas

- Disaggregate prefill and decoding onto separate GPU groups to eliminate interference.
- Co-optimize resource allocation (number of GPUs) and parallelism strategy (tensor/pipeline parallelism) independently for each phase based on TTFT and TPOT requirements.
- Place prefill and decoding instances considering cluster bandwidth to minimize communication overhead from disaggregation.

## System Design

DistServe consists of two separate serving pools: prefill GPUs and decoding GPUs. Requests are first processed by prefill GPUs to generate the initial KV cache and first token, then the KV cache is transferred to decoding GPUs for autoregressive generation. The system co-optimizes the number of GPUs and parallelism configuration for each pool given the application's TTFT and TPOT SLOs. Placement of the two pools is decided based on the cluster's network bandwidth to reduce transfer latency. A scheduler routes requests accordingly.

## Evaluation

Evaluated on popular LLMs (e.g., GPT-3, OPT) and applications (chat, summarization) with various latency requirements. Compared against state-of-the-art systems (e.g., FasterTransformer, vLLM). Headline results: DistServe serves 7.4x more requests or achieves 12.6x tighter SLO while staying within latency constraints for >90% of requests.

## Takeaways

- Disaggregating prefill and decoding is a powerful design principle for LLM serving.
- Phase-specific optimization yields significant goodput gains.
- Communication overhead from disaggregation can be mitigated by careful placement.
- The approach decouples resource allocation, enabling independent scaling of each phase.

## Related Work

DistServe is positioned against colocated serving systems like FasterTransformer, vLLM, and Orca. It addresses the interference and coupling issues inherent in those systems.

## Open Questions

How does disaggregation perform under dynamic request patterns or heterogeneous hardware? What is the optimal granularity of disaggregation (e.g., per-layer)? How to handle fault tolerance and load balancing across the two pools?
