# Federated Optimization in Heterogeneous Networks

## TL;DR

FedProx generalizes FedAvg by adding a proximal term to the local objective and allowing variable local updates, improving robustness to systems and statistical heterogeneity in federated learning.

## Problem & Motivation

Federated learning faces two key challenges: systems heterogeneity (devices have varying computational and communication capabilities) and statistical heterogeneity (data is non-i.i.d. across devices). Existing methods like FedAvg struggle with convergence and accuracy under these conditions.

## Key Ideas

- Introduce a proximal term in the local objective to stabilize training when data is non-i.i.d.
- Allow each device to perform a variable amount of local work (e.g., number of epochs) to accommodate systems heterogeneity.
- Reformulate FedAvg as a special case of FedProx with a fixed proximal term and fixed local steps.

## System Design

FedProx follows the standard federated learning structure: a central server coordinates multiple devices. In each round, the server selects a subset of devices. Each device solves a local optimization problem with a proximal term that penalizes deviation from the global model. Devices perform a variable number of local updates (e.g., SGD steps) based on their resources. The server aggregates the updated models (e.g., weighted averaging) to produce the new global model.

## Evaluation

The paper evaluates FedProx on realistic federated datasets (e.g., synthetic, FEMNIST, Shakespeare) with varying degrees of systems and statistical heterogeneity. Baselines include FedAvg and other methods. In highly heterogeneous settings, FedProx improves absolute test accuracy by 22% on average and demonstrates more stable convergence.

## Takeaways

- A simple modification (proximal term + variable local updates) can significantly improve robustness to heterogeneity.
- FedProx provides theoretical convergence guarantees under both statistical and systems heterogeneity.
- The framework is a generalization of FedAvg, making it easy to adopt.

## Related Work

FedProx is positioned as a generalization of FedAvg, the state-of-the-art method for federated learning at the time. It addresses limitations of FedAvg under heterogeneity, contrasting with other approaches that assume i.i.d. data or homogeneous systems.

## Open Questions

The paper does not explore communication efficiency or privacy guarantees. Future work could investigate combining FedProx with compression or differential privacy. Also, the optimal choice of the proximal term parameter μ is not fully characterized.
