# Federated Optimization in Heterogeneous Networks

## TL;DR

FedProx generalizes FedAvg with a proximal term to handle statistical and systems heterogeneity in federated learning, providing convergence guarantees and improving test accuracy by 22% on average in heterogeneous settings.

## Problem & Motivation

Federated learning faces two key challenges: (1) systems heterogeneity, where devices have varying computational and communication capabilities, and (2) statistical heterogeneity, where data across devices is non-identically distributed. These challenges cause standard federated optimization methods like FedAvg to suffer from unstable and slow convergence.

## Key Ideas

- Introduce a proximal term to the local objective to constrain local updates closer to the global model, mitigating the impact of heterogeneous data.
- Allow each device to perform a variable amount of local work (e.g., different numbers of local epochs) to accommodate systems heterogeneity.
- Provide theoretical convergence guarantees for non-i.i.d. data and variable local updates.

## System Design

FedProx follows the same communication structure as FedAvg: a central server coordinates multiple devices. In each round, the server sends the current global model to a subset of devices. Each device solves a local subproblem that includes a proximal term (L2 distance to the global model) and performs a variable number of local SGD steps. The server then aggregates the local model updates (e.g., by weighted averaging) to produce the new global model.

## Evaluation

The paper evaluates FedProx on realistic federated datasets (e.g., synthetic data, real-world image and text datasets) with varying degrees of statistical and systems heterogeneity. Baselines include FedAvg and other methods. Headline result: FedProx improves absolute test accuracy by 22% on average in highly heterogeneous settings, with more stable convergence.

## Takeaways

- A simple proximal modification to FedAvg significantly improves robustness to heterogeneity.
- Variable local work (systems heterogeneity) can be handled without sacrificing convergence.
- Theoretical guarantees for non-i.i.d. data are achievable with the proximal term.

## Related Work

FedProx generalizes FedAvg, the state-of-the-art method for federated learning at the time. It addresses limitations of FedAvg under heterogeneity, which prior work had not fully tackled.

## Open Questions

How to optimally tune the proximal term's strength? Can the approach scale to extremely large numbers of devices? How does it compare to other heterogeneity-handling methods like SCAFFOLD or FedNova?
