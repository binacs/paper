# Taming Request Imbalance: SLO-Aware Scheduling for Disaggregated LLM Inference

## TL;DR

Kairos is an SLO-aware scheduling system for disaggregated LLM inference that uses urgency-based priority scheduling on the prefill side and slack-guided adaptive batching on the decode side to handle request imbalance and improve SLO attainment.

## Problem & Motivation

In production LLM serving, request lengths follow a long-tail distribution, causing head-of-line blocking on the prefill side and underutilization due to stragglers on the decode side in disaggregated architectures. Current systems using FCFS scheduling for prefill and continuous batching for decode cannot adapt to this imbalance, leading to poor SLO attainment and reduced throughput.

## Key Ideas

- Urgency-based priority scheduling for prefill: predicts prefill completion times and dynamically selects requests to maximize TTFT SLO attainment.
- Slack-guided adaptive batching for decode: leverages the gap between per-step decode time and TPOT SLO to greedily pack short requests, maximizing throughput while adhering to SLOs.

## System Design

Kairos consists of two complementary mechanisms: a prefill scheduler that uses urgency-based priority scheduling, and a decode scheduler that employs slack-guided adaptive batching. The prefill scheduler predicts completion times and prioritizes requests to meet TTFT SLOs. The decode scheduler monitors slack (difference between actual decode time and TPOT SLO) and packs short requests greedily to improve throughput without violating SLOs.

## Evaluation

Evaluated using an online serving dataset and a state-of-the-art LLM. Compared to state-of-the-art baselines, Kairos improves TTFT SLO attainment by up to 23.9%, TPOT SLO attainment by up to 27.1%, end-to-end SLO attainment by up to 33.8%, and decode throughput by up to 19.3%.

## Takeaways

- Request imbalance in LLM serving can be mitigated by SLO-aware scheduling.
- Urgency-based priority scheduling effectively reduces head-of-line blocking for prefill.
- Slack-guided adaptive batching improves decode throughput while maintaining SLOs.
- Disaggregated architectures benefit from separate scheduling strategies for prefill and decode.

## Related Work

Kairos addresses limitations of current systems that use FCFS scheduling for prefill and continuous batching for decode, which are state-of-the-art baselines. It is positioned as an improvement over these approaches by introducing SLO-aware mechanisms.

## Open Questions

The paper does not discuss scalability to very large clusters or heterogeneous hardware. Future work could explore integration with other disaggregation strategies or dynamic resource allocation.
