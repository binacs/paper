# FedPLT: Scalable, Resource-Efficient, and Heterogeneity-Aware Federated Learning via Partial Layer Training

## TL;DR

FedPLT is a partial layer training method for federated learning that assigns client-specific portions of the model based on their capabilities, achieving performance comparable to full-model training while reducing trainable parameters by 71%-82%.

## Problem & Motivation

Federated learning faces high communication and computation overheads and severe device heterogeneity. Existing sub-model or partial parameter training methods suffer from inconsistent parameter distributions across clients, inaccurate global loss estimation, and increased bias and variance. There is a need for a structured approach that mimics full-model training behavior while adapting to client resource constraints.

## Key Ideas

- Propose FedPLT, a structured partial parameter training approach that trains only specific layers per client.
- Assign client-specific portions of the model according to their communication and computational capabilities.
- Combine with optimal client sampling under communication constraints to reduce sampling variance.
- Empirical analysis guides the design to exhibit training behavior similar to full model training.

## System Design

FedPLT selects a subset of layers for each client to train, based on the client's resource profile. The global model is maintained at the server. Clients train only their assigned layers and send updated parameters to the server, which aggregates them. The assignment is structured to ensure consistent parameter distributions and reduce bias/variance. Optionally, optimal client sampling is used to further improve efficiency under communication budgets.

## Evaluation

Extensive experiments compare FedPLT with full-model training (FedAvg) and existing methods. FedPLT achieves performance comparable to or surpassing FedAvg while reducing trainable parameters by 71%-82%. It outperforms existing methods in highly heterogeneous environments, adapts to client resource constraints, and reduces straggling clients.

## Takeaways

- Partial layer training can match full-model performance with significantly fewer parameters.
- Structured assignment based on client capabilities improves heterogeneity handling.
- Combining with optimal client sampling enhances communication efficiency.
- FedPLT reduces stragglers by adapting workload to client resources.

## Related Work

Prior work on sub-model training and partial parameter training addressed overheads but suffered from inconsistent distributions and increased bias/variance. FedPLT improves upon these by providing a structured approach that mimics full-model training behavior.

## Open Questions

How to automatically determine the optimal layer assignment per client? Extension to non-i.i.d. data distributions beyond heterogeneity? Impact of layer selection on convergence guarantees? Integration with other FL optimizations like compression or secure aggregation?
