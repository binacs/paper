# Position: LLM Serving Needs Mathematical Optimization and Algorithmic Foundations, Not Just Heuristics

## TL;DR

This position paper argues that LLM inference serving systems should move beyond generic heuristics (e.g., FIFO, LRU) and adopt mathematical optimization and algorithmic foundations that exploit the unique characteristics of LLM inference, such as dynamic KV cache growth and prefill-decode asymmetry.

## Problem & Motivation

Current LLM serving systems (e.g., vLLM, SGLang) rely on classical distributed computing heuristics like join-shortest-queue, round-robin, FIFO, and LRU for request routing, scheduling, and KV cache eviction. These general-purpose policies ignore the distinctive structure of LLM inference—dynamically growing KV cache memory, prefill-decode phase asymmetry, unknown output lengths, and continuous batching constraints. As a result, they may succeed in some scenarios but fail unpredictably in others, lacking provable performance guarantees.

## Key Ideas

- Develop mathematical models that capture LLM inference characteristics (dynamic KV cache, prefill-decode asymmetry, unknown output lengths, continuous batching).
- Design algorithms with provable performance guarantees across diverse workloads, rather than relying on heuristics.
- Leverage operations research and ML systems intersection to create principled methods that match or exceed heuristic performance.

## Evaluation

The paper does not present experimental evaluation; it is a position paper. It references emerging work showing principled methods can match or exceed heuristic performance while providing theoretical guarantees.

## Takeaways

- LLM serving has outgrown generic heuristics; mathematical optimization and algorithmic foundations are needed.
- Key LLM inference characteristics (dynamic KV cache, prefill-decode asymmetry, unknown output lengths, continuous batching) must be modeled.
- Principled methods from operations research can provide provable performance guarantees.
- The community should recognize algorithmic design for LLM serving as a research frontier.

## Related Work

The paper contrasts with existing serving systems like vLLM and SGLang, which still use classical heuristics. It positions itself as a call to move beyond these heuristics toward mathematical optimization.

## Open Questions

- What specific mathematical models best capture LLM inference characteristics?
- How to design algorithms with provable guarantees that are also practical and efficient?
- How to handle the trade-off between theoretical optimality and real-world implementation constraints?
