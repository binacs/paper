# Serverless in the Wild: Characterizing and Optimizing the Serverless Workload at a Large Cloud Provider

## TL;DR

This paper characterizes the entire production FaaS workload of Azure Functions, revealing that most functions are invoked infrequently with an 8-order-of-magnitude range in invocation frequencies, and proposes a practical resource management policy that reduces cold starts while using fewer resources than existing policies.

## Problem & Motivation

Cloud providers offering Function-as-a-Service (FaaS) must provide the illusion of always-available resources (fast invocations without cold starts) while minimizing resource cost. However, there is little public information on FaaS workload characteristics, making it difficult to design efficient resource management policies. The paper addresses this gap by characterizing a large-scale production FaaS workload and using the insights to optimize resource allocation.

## Key Ideas

- Characterization of the entire Azure Functions production workload, revealing invocation frequency distribution spanning 8 orders of magnitude.
- Observation that most functions are invoked very infrequently, leading to high potential for cold starts.
- Proposal of a practical resource management policy that reduces cold starts by keeping warm resources for frequently invoked functions while releasing resources for infrequent ones.
- The policy spends fewer resources than state-of-the-practice policies by leveraging workload patterns.

## System Design

The paper does not introduce a new system but describes a resource management policy for existing FaaS platforms. The policy likely involves monitoring invocation frequencies and dynamically adjusting the number of warm containers per function, keeping more warm for frequent functions and fewer for infrequent ones. The control flow would involve a resource manager that tracks function invocation patterns and decides when to keep or release containers.

## Evaluation

The paper validates its claims by characterizing the Azure Functions workload and comparing the proposed policy against state-of-the-practice policies. Headline numbers include a significant reduction in cold starts while using fewer resources, though specific percentages are not provided in the abstract.

## Takeaways

- FaaS workloads exhibit extreme heterogeneity in invocation frequencies, with most functions invoked rarely.
- Resource management policies must account for this heterogeneity to balance cold starts and resource cost.
- Keeping warm resources for frequently invoked functions and releasing them for infrequent ones can reduce cold starts without increasing resource usage.
- Real-world workload characterization is crucial for designing efficient cloud resource management.

## Related Work

The paper addresses the lack of public information on FaaS workload characteristics, contrasting with prior work that either focuses on other cloud paradigms or lacks production-scale data. It positions itself as the first large-scale characterization of a production FaaS workload.

- How does the policy handle functions with varying execution times or resource requirements?
