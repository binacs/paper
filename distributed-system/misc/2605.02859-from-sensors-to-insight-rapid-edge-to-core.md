# From Sensors to Insight: Rapid, Edge-to-Core Application Development for Sensor-Driven Applications

## TL;DR

This paper presents an experience-driven methodology combining pattern-based workflow engineering and AI assistance to rapidly develop sensor-driven applications across the edge-to-cloud continuum, using Pegasus on FABRIC and case studies in air quality, earthquake, and soil moisture monitoring.

## Problem & Motivation

Transforming raw sensor data into actionable insights across heterogeneous edge-to-cloud infrastructure is challenging. Scientists lack the cross-domain expertise needed to provision and manage execution on emerging platforms like Data Processing Units, which hinders rapid prototyping and iterative exploration of sensor-driven applications.

## Key Ideas

- Pattern-based workflow engineering: reuse and adapt existing workflows (e.g., Orcasound hydrophone) as templates for new domains.
- AI-assisted development: leverage AI to generate and refine workflow structures.
- Modular configuration and placement: extend abstract workflows to edge resources via modular components and placement decisions.
- Experience-driven methodology: focus on user productivity and practical lessons rather than peak performance.

## System Design

The methodology is implemented using Pegasus workflow management system on the FABRIC testbed. It starts with a reusable template (Orcasound hydrophone workflow). Patterns are extracted and applied to generate workflows for air quality, earthquake, and soil moisture monitoring. AI assists in refining these abstract structures. Modular configuration allows deployment on edge resources by adjusting components and placement. The control flow follows a pattern-based generation step, then AI refinement, then modular edge extension.

## Evaluation

Evaluation focuses on user productivity and practical lessons through case studies in air quality, earthquake, and soil moisture monitoring. No specific performance numbers are reported; instead, the paper illustrates how the methodology lowers the entry barrier for non-experts and enables iterative exploration.

## Takeaways

- Pattern-based reuse of workflows accelerates development across sensor domains.
- AI assistance can lower the barrier for non-experts in distributed infrastructure.
- Modular configuration enables flexible edge-to-cloud deployment.
- User productivity and iterative exploration are more important than peak performance in rapid prototyping.

## Related Work

The paper builds on Pegasus workflow system and FABRIC testbed. It positions itself against the difficulty of provisioning heterogeneous infrastructure and managing execution on emerging platforms like Data Processing Units, which typically require cross-domain expertise.

## Open Questions

- How does the methodology scale to more complex sensor networks or real-time constraints?
- What is the quantitative improvement in development time compared to traditional approaches?
- Can the AI assistance be generalized to other workflow systems beyond Pegasus?
- How does the approach handle security and privacy concerns in edge deployments?
