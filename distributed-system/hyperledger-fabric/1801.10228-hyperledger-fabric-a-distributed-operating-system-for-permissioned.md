# Hyperledger fabric: a distributed operating system for permissioned blockchains

## TL;DR

Hyperledger Fabric is a modular, extensible permissioned blockchain platform that supports general-purpose smart contracts without a native cryptocurrency, achieving over 3500 transactions per second with sub-second latency.

## Problem & Motivation

Existing permissioned blockchain platforms are often rigid, requiring domain-specific languages for smart contracts and depending on a native cryptocurrency. They also struggle with non-determinism, resource exhaustion, and performance attacks. Fabric addresses these issues by providing a flexible, modular architecture that can be tailored to different trust models and use cases.

## Key Ideas

- Modular consensus protocols allowing pluggable trust models.
- Smart contracts in standard programming languages (e.g., Go, Java) without cryptocurrency.
- Portable membership model integrated with industry-standard identity management.
- Novel blockchain design to handle non-determinism and resource exhaustion.
- Execute-order-validate architecture separating transaction execution from ordering.

## System Design

Fabric consists of peers (endorsers and committers), ordering service (consensus), and membership service provider (MSP). Transactions are executed by endorsing peers, then ordered by the ordering service, and finally validated and committed by committing peers. The system supports channels for private data and uses a key-value store (LevelDB or CouchDB) for world state.

## Evaluation

Evaluated with a Bitcoin-inspired digital currency benchmark. Achieves end-to-end throughput >3500 transactions per second in popular deployment configurations, with sub-second latency, scaling to over 100 peers.

## Takeaways

- Modular consensus enables deployment in diverse trust environments.
- No native cryptocurrency reduces complexity and regulatory friction.
- General-purpose smart contracts lower the barrier for developers.
- Execute-order-validate paradigm improves performance and scalability.
- Permissioned model with portable membership enhances enterprise adoption.

## Related Work

Fabric is contrasted with existing blockchain platforms that require domain-specific languages or rely on a cryptocurrency, such as Ethereum and Bitcoin. It is the first extensible permissioned blockchain system for distributed applications.

## Open Questions

Limitations include potential performance bottlenecks in the ordering service and complexity of channel management. Future work may explore more efficient consensus protocols, improved privacy mechanisms, and integration with other Hyperledger projects.
